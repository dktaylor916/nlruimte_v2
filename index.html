Allocation of Public Space in the Netherlands

Who does public space really belong to? When cities are designed, public space is allocated between different types of users. This allocation of the built enviroment decides which users will be favored, and who in the city is prioritized in the planning the process. But how can this be calculated?

In this project, a dataset containing information on all public space in the Netherlands is used. All public space is categorized by its main function and user (cars, parking, cyclists, and pedestrians)The total area of each category is then calculated in order to make a direct comparison between the allocation of space. This calculation is made at 3 different levels (Gemeente (City), Wijk (Neighborhood), Buurt (Block)).
